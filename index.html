FORESTS

INTRODUCTION

A forest is a large area of land covered mainly with trees and other plants.

It is a natural home for many animals, birds, and insects, and it helps keep the air clean by producing oxygen and absorbing carbon dioxide.

TYPES OF FORESTS

  • Tropical rainforest
  • Temperate forest
  • Boreal forest

TROPICAL RAINFORESTS

A tropical rainforest is a hot, humid, and wet biome located near the Earth's equator.
These forests are characterized by their lush, broad-leaved trees that form a dense upper canopy and contain an unparalleled diversity of life,
representing Earth's oldest major vegetation type.

TEMPERATE FOREST

A temperate forest is a biome characterized by a moderate climate, distinct seasons,
and a variety of trees and animal life. The biome is located primarily in the mid-latitude regions
of both the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.

BOREAL FOREST

The boreal forest, or taiga, is the world's largest terrestrial biome, a cold-climate forest that
forms a circumpolar ring across the northern regions of North America, Europe, and Asia.
It is characterized by long, cold winters; short, mild summers; and an abundance of coniferous trees.

IMPORTANCE OF FORESTS

  1. Climate Regulation
  2. Water Cycle
  3. Soil and Air Quality
  4. Habitat for Wildlife
  5. Food and Resources
  6. Economic Value
  7. Health Benefits
